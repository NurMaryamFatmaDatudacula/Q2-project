Paw Facts

Despite their seemingly sullen expression, Persian cats are actually the sweetest and most laid-back cats around. When introduced as kittens, the fancy cats are lovable and endearing animals that would get along with the whole family. But Persian cats do need special care; learn more about them; beauty is not always easy. The Persian cat is a medium-sized, stockily built animal with a round, broad head that is somewhat large in relation to the body, large round eyes, and a short muzzle that gives them a unique, grumpy expression. The small size and round shape of the face are further enhanced by the wide-set, tiny ears. The compact body features large round paws, short, sturdy legs, and a short, bushy tail. There is a soft, dense undercoat beneath the long, luxurious coat, which is incredibly thick. The Persian Longhair, who is kind and gentle, values a quiet house where they can unwind and feel safe. Rather than making a mawkish or yowling cry, their voice is melodious and pleasant. They are a quiet cat. They can express their happiness with a rumbling purr and a beam of pleasure, or their confusion at human stupidity with a knowing stare and a tilt of the head thanks to their expressive large eyes. Instead of being active climbers and jumpers, they are more of a decorative cat that likes to lounge in the sun and drape themselves creatively over furniture.
